Horses (Equus ferus caballus) are large, herbivorous, four-legged mammals with a single-toed, hoofed foot, known for their strength, speed, and long association with humans. They have long, muscular necks, long heads, and mane hair, with heights typically around 1.5 meters (5 feet) at the shoulder. They are social, intelligent, and highly sensitive.

Horses are fascinating, social creatures that can sleep standing up, possess nearly 360-degree vision, and communicate complex emotions through ear position and body language. Key quirks include their inability to breathe through their mouths or vomit, and their ability to run just hours after birth. They are highly intelligent, sensitive animals with 205 bones.
